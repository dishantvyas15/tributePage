Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
Proudly regarded as the 'Missile Man of India', Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam devoted his entire life serving the nation.
A brief time-line of his life (1931-2015):

  • 1931:
    Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India (then, Madras Presidency, British India)

  • 1955:
    Enrolled for aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology

  • 1960:
    Graduated and joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of DRDO as a scientist

  • 1965:
    Started working independently on expandable rocket project at DRDO

  • 1696:
    Joined the Indian Space Research Organization as the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)

  • 1970:
    Started working in the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

  • 1981:
    Was honoured with the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India

  • 1990:
    Was honoured with the Padma Vibhushan by the Government of India

  • 1997:
    Received the highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna from the Government of India

  • 2002:
    Became the 11th President of India

  • 2015:
    Departed for Heaven
